Will 2024 see both Biden and Trump running for president again?
The rundown:

Biden has recently confirmed his intentions to run again in 2024 and though Trump seems to still be on the fence, the ticket could end up looking like it did in 2020. But with the Biden administration putting VP Harris front and center, it could also end up looking like a very different campaign altogether.


Why it matters:

Biden’s age has been a subject of criticism from Republicans and Trump has so far only been a one-term president, so others seem to be gearing up to take their places in their respective parties. The question that might matter the most, though, is how the Republican Party can fare in a post-Trump world.

While transgender rights are making gains at the federal level, states are pushing back
The rundown:

Though 2021 has brought the transgender community some victories thanks to the new Biden administration, it has continued to be a tough year for trans rights at the state level. There are currently multiple state bills being considered that would limit medical access for trans youth and bar trans athletes from competing according to their gender identity.


Why it matters:

While Joe Biden is making a point of being an ally to the LGBTQ community, state legislatures can still pass anti-trans legislation so long as more comprehensive pro-trans legislation hasn’t been passed at the federal level. Many of these proposed laws will likely set up legal battles that could force the Supreme Court to rule more directly on what constitutes discrimination based on gender identity.

Everything you need to know about the new Georgia voting law
The rundown:

A new law signed by Georgia’s governor would dramatically reshape the state’s elections going forward. While the creators of the new law say they created it to help ensure election integrity, critics, including US President Joe Biden, argue the law is designed to make it harder for Black Americans and other minorities to vote.


Why it matters:

Unsupported claims of widespread election fraud in the 2020 US presidential election have led multiple Republican-led states to revise their voting laws. The law may force Democrats, many of whom fear such laws are being used to restrict the rights of people of color, to unify behind a federal election law that would make voting easier.
